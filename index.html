ThumbJoy Joystick — Finding the Right Base
The Kai Concepts ThumbJoy uses an APEM HF55 as its base joystick, but it has critical problems: the spring is too soft (1.3N breakout = no feedback at foiling speeds), it's NOT actually waterproof despite IP67 claims, and the hollow shaft is too tight for 6 Melexis wires. This overview maps the market for better alternatives.
📋 Must-Have Requirements
⬤
Hollow Shaft for Wire Routing
DEAL BREAKER — 6 Melexis wires must route through shaft to thumbwheel in grip
⬤
Stiffer Spring / Force Feedback
Current HF55 at 1.3N is way too light — need confident center-feel at 25+ knots
⬤
True Waterproof (IP67+)
APEM's IP67 claim is false. Need real marine sealing — splashes, spray, submersion.
⬤
Z-Axis Twist (Spring Return)
Used for steering left/right — spring-return to center essential
⬤
CAN J1939 Interface
500kbps CAN preferred, or hall effect analog feeding Teensy CAN
⬤
Compact / Fits ThumbJoy Housing
Beer-can sized cylindrical base — shallow behind-panel depth

Opportunity: First-Ever Volvo Penta Marine Joystick Teardown
The Volvo Penta EVC2 unit (P/N 24011) found in the Kai Concepts shop could be the FIRST public teardown of a premium marine joystick. No one has ever documented the internal mechanism of a Volvo Penta, Mercury, Dockmate, or any other marine joystick. Opening this unit would reveal how the world's #1 marine joystick maker solved spring force, sealing, twist axis, and hall sensor placement.

📌 Decision Matrix Notes
  • ● JR Merritt CS3 — Best overall: marine-rated, configurable springs, twist + thumbwheel. MUST verify hollow shaft.
  • ● Elobau J4F — True IP67, modular handles, German quality. Strong #2 pick. Verify shaft dimensions.
  • ● RunnTech RT600 — Best prototyping pick ($50-150). Test the 3-axis twist concept quickly before committing $500+ on industrial units.
  • ● Alternative path: If no joystick has hollow shaft, consider through-wall magnetic sensing (Melexis below grip, magnet through housing wall) to eliminate wire routing entirely.
