ThumbJoy Joystick — Finding the Right Base
The Kai Concepts ThumbJoy uses an APEM HF55 as its base joystick, but it has critical problems: the spring is too soft (1.3N breakout = no feedback at foiling speeds), it's NOT actually waterproof despite IP67 claims, and the hollow shaft is too tight for 6 Melexis wires. This overview maps the market for better alternatives.
📋 Requirements (Updated 2026-03-31)
⬤
1. IP67 Waterproof (MANDATORY)
Must be genuinely IP67 rated. No exceptions — marine environment, spray, splash, submersion.
⬤
2. Vertical Thumbwheel on Top
Must have a vertical thumbwheel in the grip top, OR the ability to add our own Melexis thumbwheel — which means cables down the shaft or reusing existing internal wires.
⬤
3. X/Y Axes with Y-Gate Option
Y = forward/backward axis. X axis optionally lockable via Y-gate (restrict to Y-only movement when needed).
⬤
4. Z-Axis Rotation
Twist/rotation axis available for steering — spring-return to center.
⬤
5. Palm-Sized Compact
Must fit in a palm with thumb resting on thumbwheel. Not too large — fingertip/hand-grip class.
⬤
6. Optional 2–4 Buttons
Optional push buttons on grip (2–4). Not required but nice to have for mode switching.
⬤
7. Analog or CAN — All Works
CAN J1939, CANopen, or analog hall effect output. All acceptable — Teensy can read any of them.

📌 Decision Matrix Notes
  • ●Ruffy HE3 — True IP67, dual redundant sensors, proven in Opener BlackFly eVTOL. Confirmed hollow shaft. $281 for 3-axis. Top pick.
  • ●Elobau J4F — 🔬 ACTIVE EVALUATION (Merten configuring in Elobau tool, Mar 31). IP67, CAN J1939, capacitive presence detection. German quality.
  • ●OTTO JHT Z-axis — IP68S (submersible!). 2.2–6.7N operating force. Miniature form factor.
  • ●MEGATRON TRY120 — NEW: Purpose-built for marine bow thruster control. IP67, CAN J1939, 26mm depth, 9Nm Z-torque. Check hollow shaft.
  • ●JR Merritt CS3/VCS2 — ❌ REJECTED: IP65 only, fails mandatory IP67 waterproofing requirement.
  • ●Alternative path: If no joystick has hollow shaft, consider through-wall magnetic sensing (Melexis below grip, magnet through housing wall) to eliminate wire routing entirely.
